    <section id="history">
        <h2>History of the Periodic Table</h2>
        <p><strong>Dmitri Mendeleev:</strong> Developed the first periodic table and predicted undiscovered elements.</p>
        <p><strong>Henry Moseley:</strong> Reorganized the table by atomic number, leading to the modern periodic table.</p>
    </section>

    <section id="modern-table">
        <h2>Modern Periodic Table Organization</h2>
        <p><strong>Groups and Periods:</strong> The periodic table is organized by rows (periods) and columns (groups).</p>
        <p><strong>Valence Electrons:</strong> The number of valence electrons helps predict element behavior.</p>
        <p><strong>Sublevels (S, P, D, F blocks):</strong> The periodic table is divided into blocks based on electron configurations.</p>
    </section>

    <section id="trends">
        <h2>Periodic Trends</h2>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Atomic Radius:</strong> Decreases across a period, increases down a group.</li>
            <li><strong>Ionic Radius:</strong> Similar trend to atomic radius.</li>
            <li><strong>Ionization Energy:</strong> Increases across a period, decreases down a group.</li>
            <li><strong>Electronegativity:</strong> Increases across a period, decreases down a group.</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="families">
        <h2>Element Families</h2>
        <h3>S Block Elements</h3>
        <p><strong>Hydrogen, Alkali Metals, Alkaline Earth Metals:</strong> Highly reactive elements, especially in the alkali group.</p>

        <h3>P Block Elements</h3>
        <p><strong>Boron Group, Carbon Group, Nitrogen Group, Oxygen Group, Halogens, Noble Gases:</strong> Diverse group including metals, metalloids, and nonmetals.</p>

        <h3>D Block Elements</h3>
        <p><strong>Transition Metals:</strong> Known for their conductivity and wide range of oxidation states.</p>

        <h3>F Block Elements</h3>
        <p><strong>Lanthanides and Actinides:</strong> Rare earth elements and radioactive elements found in the f-block.</p>
    </section>
